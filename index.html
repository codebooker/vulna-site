Vulna is a self-hosted, distributed security-assessment platform. A central dashboard coordinates a fleet of lightweight scout probes across your sites and network segments, driving the scanners you already trust. No cloud account, no license key, no findings leaving your network.

The orchestration, safety, and reporting layer around the tools you already trust.

Vulna is not another vulnerability engine. It coordinates proven open-source scanners, keeps them inside approved scope, and turns their raw output into correlated assets, tracked findings, and shareable reports.

01

One control plane

VulnaDash runs discovery, scanning, findings, and reporting for every scope from a single dashboard, REST API, and CLI. No SSH-ing into ten scanner boxes at 1am to piece together what changed.

02

Scouts reach every segment

VulnaScout is a small Go agent that runs on a Raspberry Pi, a VM, a container, or an old desktop. Put one in each site, VLAN, or DMZ and assess networks a single scanner box could never see.

03

A layer, not another engine

Vulna drives nmap, Nuclei, testssl.sh, and OWASP ZAP, then normalizes, dedupes, and correlates the results. You get the safety, evidence, and reporting on top of engines you can already audit.

04

Safe by design

Every scout is scope-gated and scans nothing until you approve a network scope. Jobs and policy are Ed25519-signed, probes speak mutual TLS, and active and pentest modules stay allowlisted behind approval gates.

05

CVE intelligence built in

VulnaWatch syncs NVD, CISA KEV, and EPSS, enriches every finding, and raises a watch event the moment an asset you already track becomes newly exploitable.

06

From finding to fixed

Assign owners, set due dates, accept risk with an expiry, and rescan a single finding. Vulna auto-resolves and reopens, then exports executive and technical PDFs, CSVs, and signed JSON bundles.

Runs on what you already own.

No certified hardware list and no minimum spend. The dashboard needs a modest always-on host; scouts are happy on almost anything that boots Linux.

ComponentCPURAMDiskTypical hardware
VulnaDashone per deployment 2 cores 4 GB 20 GB A mini PC, a NUC, a VM on your hypervisor, or a small cloud instance. Ships as a Docker Compose stack.
VulnaScoutas many as you need 1 core 512 MB 1 GB Raspberry Pi 3B+ and newer, any VM or container, thin clients, or the 2011 desktop in the closet.

Official builds for linux/amd64 and linux/arm64. VulnaDash installs with the signed vulna CLI or a Compose stack and migrates its own database on start. A scout is one binary plus one systemd unit, enrolled over mutual TLS with a single-use join token.

From zero to first scan.

Stand up the dashboard, enroll a scout, approve a scope. The long version covering reverse proxies, TLS, and air-gapped installs lives in the docs.

step 1 / dashboard

Stand up VulnaDash

# download and verify the signed release
$ curl -fsSLO vulna.dev/install.sh
$ sh install.sh -- install
✓ dashboard up on :8443
step 2 / scout

Enroll a scout

# on the Pi / VM / remote host
$ ./install-scout.sh \
    --token <JOIN-TOKEN>
✓ enrolled, scope-gated
step 3 / scope

Approve a scope and scan

$ vulna doctor
COMPONENT    STATUS
dashboard    ok
local scout  ok
scout-dmz    ok
# approve a scope, pick a preset

Stuck on a reverse proxy, a strange NAT, or an air-gapped site? The docs cover it, and the issue tracker is answered by humans.

Open source, the whole stack.

Vulna is AGPL-3.0 and developed in the open on GitHub. No cloud tier, no open-core bait, no capability held back for an enterprise plan. Self-hosted means the findings, the evidence, and the keys stay on your infrastructure.

Bug reports, pull requests, and "it broke on my weird hardware" issues are all welcome. Good first issues are labeled.
